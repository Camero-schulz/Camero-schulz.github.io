For the Ancient Egyptians, the dream world was what existed between the living and the dead. When one enters a dream, the Egyptians thought they were communicating with the dead. You would then have to interpret the dream to understand what the dream entities were trying to say.

The Ancient Hebrews believed that sleep was the veil between the living and the world of angels, spirits and demons. Sometimes, God himself would speak through dreams. Those who would follow the Old Testament viewed dreams as divine wisdom.

Native Americans believed that dreams were it's own seperate dimension where you could interact with a universal consciousness. There was knowledge to be learnt from the spirits and animals that accompany it.

Through all of these different cultures, there exists a common experience that considers the dream space as a sacred realm. Where the messages given to them by higher orders, are not to be overlooked, but cherished and integrated in every day life.

The Real Reason

Why Do We Sleep?

Although there are a lot of unknowns to why we sleep. From what we understand, sleep serves multiple purposes rather than a single function. Over the years, scientists have proposed numerous theories to explain the phenomenon of sleep. These theories encompass various ideas, including:

  1. Energy Conservation - Sleeping just 8 hours can conserve up to 35% of caloric needs.
  2. Cellular Restoration - Sleep allows cells to repair and grow.
  3. Brain Function - Lack of sleep negatively effects memory and problem-solving abilities. Sleep is also useful for converting short-term memories into long-turn memories. Not getting enough sleep could potentially result in lasting brain damage.
  4. Emotional Stability - While sleeping, brain activity increases in areas that regulate mood - mainly the amygdala. When sleep-deprived, the amygdala is more likely to overreact in stressful situations. A lack of sleep is linked to numerous mental health problems, including, depression, anxiety and paranoia
  5. Healthy Immunity - Improper sleep can weaken the immune system and make you more susceptible to germs.

Why Do We Dream?

Dreaming is a little more complicated. The truth is, even after years of research and scientifc innovation, no one really knows why we dream. Although numerous theories have been proposed, none have provided conclusive explanations. These theories encompass various ideas.

Sigmund Freud regarded dreams as the road to the unconcious mind. According to Freud, dreams occur due to "stimuli from the external world, subjective experiences, organic stimuli within the body, and mental activities during sleep". He believed that the connections the dream makes is not random, but rather, one's unconcious desires. The goal of the dream is to interpret and reveal what it truly means, no matter how pleasent or unpleasent the dream is.

More modern theories suggest that dreams could have other meanings. Maybe dreams help aid in memory function. Maybe they exist to make us more creative. Maybe we dream to help us prepare and protect for the worst.

However, the most believed reason why we dream is due to how our brains process memories. While sleeping, our brain is busy strengthening connections and converting short-term memory into long-term memory. As the theory goes, while completing these tasks, our brain sends electrical signals that gets detected by our conscious brain. Our consciousness doesn't understand what the signals means, trys to fill in the gaps the best it can, and the next thing we know, we're dreaming.
